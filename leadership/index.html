Leadership Characteristics

Organised

Part of being a successful leader is that you need to be organised. Leading people is not something that happens by accident. Rather, it is a planning for, and shaping a set of desirable behaviours that create an environment where people want to work and follow you as a leader.

Forward-thinking

To become a better leader, you'll want to develop your capacity to envision the future. Focusing on the future sets leaders apart. The capacity to imagine and communicate exciting future possibilities is a defining characteristic and can be developed over time.

Relationship-building

Building relationships is one of the strongest skill-sets related to leadership effectiveness. Building and maintaining relationships with team members further motivates others to work well together and create an environment of open and honest sharing which, enhances team cohesion and effectiveness.

Inspirational

The ability to inspire people to reach great heights of performance and success is a essential leadership skill. Passion, purpose, listening and meaning help make a leader inspirational. The ability to communicate enthusiasm, purpose and meaning to others helps establish an inspirational “can do” culture.

Knowledgeable

Knowledgeable leaders must have an extensive range of information and knowledge at their disposal. This means they should stay current by reading widely and seek to transfer this knowledge effectively to the teams they lead. It is easier for people to trust someone who is up to date with current business practices.

Based on your response, you may want to work on the following areas.

Organised

Being well organised is a key skill to effective leadership. This is a skill that can be developed and practiced over time and strategies can be put into place to further advance your ability to be organised. Team members tend to respond well to a leader who is well organised and has "everything in place" as a structured approach to completing a project, enables team members to work with in boundaries set to reach objectives.

Forward-thinking

A leader who is forward-thinking is one who has the ability to see beyond current challenges and look into the future. Being forward thinking enables you to have greater vision of the potentials and possibilities for the future. It also requires you to look for resolutions or opportunities in new ways and keep up with current trends. Forward thinking is also about being open-minded to new possibilities, diverse ideas and unconventional methods in your approach to leading and managing.

Relationship-building

An effective leader is one who builds appropriate, positive relationships with those she or he works with. The foundation of any good working relationship is trust and transparency. A leader who can be trusted to make the right decisions at the right time and be transparent in the delivery of information to and expectations of the team, encourages the same interaction from team members. Building solid relationships with team members promotes individual and team development resulting in a positive impact on project outcomes.

Inspirational

Inspirational leadership is about seeing yourself as a role model and as an example to others. You are the person who sets the standards that your team follows. A key characteristic of being inspiring and motivating others is to set high standards and expectations and to be continuously encouraging of the team's efforts. An inspirational leader is one who creates enthusiasm, empowers others and instills confidence in those they lead.

Knowledgeable

A knowledgeable leader is one who has depth and breadth of facts, experience, and information in an industry, area or project they are involved in. Building knowledge and experience within an area of expertise brings stability to the team and the project as a whole. A knowledgeable leader makes decisions that are evidence-based and is able to advise and respond to people and challenges appropriately.
