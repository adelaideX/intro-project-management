Low risk project

In this situation you can proceed with the project as planned. Although you will take all of the necessary precautions while engaging in the project, the project environment (PESTLE) appears to be well known and risks understood. Ensure stakeholders are kept in the loop during execution.

Medium risk project

Review the risk categories in your PESTLE rating/score that have higher scores. These categories will point towards potential challenges and risks in executing your project. Try to identify ways to reduce or eliminate these risks before proceeding. Communicate with stakeholders and your team to consider different approaches to respond to these risks.

Higher risk project

If your project reflects a higher risk profile, it is often good idea to revisit your project idea/concept and explore alternative options by applying some critical and creative thinking techniques. In such a situation, take time to rethink your approach and then re-do the PESTLE profile. Before proceeding with your project, it is advisable that you take sufficient time to engage with your team and broader stakeholders to identify appropriate risk responses to reduce the likelihood and impact of these risks on your project during execution.

High risk project

In a high-risk project, take a step back and consider all of the alternatives before making a decision to continue with the project. Should you decide to proceed, you will need to ensure that the identified high risks categories and items are adequately addressed and appropriate contingencies are in put into place. Such risks will require constant monitoring and review to ensure you remain on top of various mitigation actions. Good communication with your project team and other stakeholders is essential. Where the risk profile indicates an extremely high risk, one option might be to avoid the project entirely. Alternately, reconsider all of the planning and seek non-traditional approaches in order to consider the project in a different way, where risks might be better understood or where risks can be reduced - or for instance eliminated by transferring them to other parties (such as experienced contractors).
